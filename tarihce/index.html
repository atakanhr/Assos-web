B.C. 13th century According to The Hitit sources, Assuwa (?) and Pedasos (?) can be related to Assos
B.C. 7th Century Lesbian Methymnians found the city Assos
B.C. 560-546 Lydian Sovereignty (?)
B.C. 546 Anatolia's entry into the Persian Kingdom
B.C. 540-530 Construction of the Temple of Athena
B.C. 478 / 77-412 Assos joined the Attic Delos Naval Union
B.C. 365 Assos was besieged by the Persian satrap Ariobarzanes
B.C. 365 Banker Eubulos seized Assos and Aterneus
B.C. 350 The tyranny of Hermias, slave of Eubulos

B.C. 345 Persians killed Hermias, as a result, the philosophers left Assos.
B.C. 334 Alexander the Great entered Anatolia and liberated the Troad region.
B.C. 331 Birth of the Stoic Philosopher Kleanthes in Assos
B.C. 241 Domination of the Kingdom of Pergamon
B.C. 204 Sodamos of Assos wins the 144th Olympic games
B.C. 133 Pergamon kingdom leaves Western Anatolia to the Roman State
A.D. 16 Germanicus and his wife Agrippina visit Assos
A.D. 56/57 Apostle St. Paul visit Assos
A.D. 330 Constantine I founded the capital of Constantinople
A.D. 395-1453 Domination of the Eastern Roman (Byzantine) Empire
1080 Seljuk Sultan Suleiman's capture of the Troad region
1081-1097 Tshaka Bey's capture of the cities of Troad Region
1097 Emperor Ioannes Dukas reintroduces Troad to Byzantine territory
1204-1224 The capture of the Troad region by the Latins
1261 Troad re-entry into the Byzantine Empire
Early 14th century Capture of the southern part of the Troad by the Turks, construction of the mosque and the bridge.

1981-2005 Turkish Excavations under the direction of Prof. Dr. Ümit Serdaroğlu
